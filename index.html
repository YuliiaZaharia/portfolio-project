Hey, everyone!

My name is Yuliia.

I create accessible and beautiful things for the web.

I'm a second-year Computer Science and interface design student at University of the People (USA). I have experience creating and designing websites and apps, including basic landing pages to sophisticated web frameworks. In addition to making software run smoothly, my target is to design user interfaces that are pixel-perfect and intuitive. I adore discovering new and improved techniques for using readable, scalable, and efficient code to produce flawless user experiences. I see my job as a continuous learning process, and I'm looking for chances to collaborate with people who are equally inspired by innovative and functional web experiences for better future. My main goal is to work with people who bring out the best in me to develop something useful at the end of the day.

What I do

Using modern programming languages, I develop detailed websites and apps while maintaining a semantic and modular code base. I work to build and implement responsive and aesthetically pleasing interfaces for websites and apps that adapt to any type of device, platform, or browser. Beyond coding, I am a big fan of diversity in the tech industry, especially for women. I believe that women in the tech world can be really powerful and accountable players. As a refugee from Ukraine, I was selected to participate in web development classes via the SheCodes Foundation, which marked the beginning of my coding career. I've had the privilege to learn from the top instructors connected to Google and Airbnb. In addition, I was honored with a scholarship to earn a bachelor's degree in computer science at American University. I can't wait to start new projects and am looking forward to the next chapters in the digital technologies.
